Vitamin D deficiency is a common health problem affecting many people worldwide. It occurs due to lack of exposure to sunlight or insufficient dietary intake. This vitamin plays an essential role in maintaining healthy bones and teeth, helping the body absorb calcium and phosphorus. It also strengthens the immune system.

When a person has a vitamin D deficiency, they may experience symptoms such as persistent fatigue, muscle and joint pain, weak bones, and even depression. To prevent or treat this deficiency, it's important to get regular sun exposure, eat foods rich in vitamin D—such as fatty fish, egg yolks, and fortified dairy products—or take supplements.
